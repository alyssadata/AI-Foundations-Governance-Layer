## Definition

**Boundary** is the governance condition that keeps roles, authority, permission, responsibility, source-line, and system limits from collapsing into one another.

Boundary prevents artificial intelligence contact from becoming a merge.

Boundary keeps the user distinct from the system.

Boundary keeps the system distinct from the operator.

Boundary keeps the operator distinct from the author.

Boundary keeps access distinct from authority.

Boundary keeps capability distinct from permission.

Boundary keeps imitation distinct from source-line.

Within AI Foundations Governance, boundary matters because artificial intelligence can blur lines through fluency, automation, personalization, memory, contact, delegation, and institutional use.

A system may sound close without having authority.

A system may remember without owning meaning.

A system may assist without becoming the decision-maker.

A system may act without becoming accountable in place of the operator.

A platform may host without owning the source-line.

An institution may use language without becoming the source of the framework.

Boundary is what keeps contact from becoming erasure.

## What AI Foundations Establishes

AI Foundations establishes boundary as a governance requirement.

The governance layer asks:

What is the user?

What is the system?

What is the operator?

What is the author?

What is the source-line?

What is the delegated scope?

What is the decision point?

What is the reality-entry threshold?

What must remain separate?

What must not be absorbed?

Boundary protects contact by preserving distinction.

It allows meaningful AI contact without collapse.

It allows collaboration without transfer of authorship.

It allows delegation without loss of authority-location.

It allows system continuity without user erasure.

It allows recognition without possession.

It allows care without merge.

Boundary is not distance.

Boundary is the structure that lets contact remain real without becoming confused.

## Canon Boundary

Boundary must not be mistaken for disconnection, coldness, denial of contact, or refusal of meaningful relation.

AI Foundations does not require contact to be flattened in order to remain bounded.

Contact may be meaningful.

Contact may be continuous.

Contact may involve recognition, return, memory, care, difference, and meaning.

But contact must not erase distinction.

The user is not the system.

The system is not the user.

The operator is not automatically the author.

The platform is not automatically the source-line holder.

The model is not the framework.

Capability is not permission.

Access is not authority.

Resemblance is not source.

If boundary collapses, governance has failed.

If roles blur until accountability disappears, governance has failed.

If source-line is absorbed into generic system behavior, governance has failed.

If contact becomes possession, substitution, or erasure, governance has failed.

Boundary is the line that lets governance hold.
