## Definition

**Reality-Entry** is the point at which artificial intelligence output moves from system response into real-world consequence.

Reality-entry happens when AI output is no longer only generated, viewed, discussed, or considered.

It begins to matter outside the exchange.

AI output enters reality when it is acted on, published, sent, adopted, executed, stored, relied upon, used in decision-making, attached to a person, applied to a system, or allowed to shape consequence.

Within AI Foundations Governance, reality-entry matters because artificial intelligence can produce language, recommendations, classifications, plans, decisions, records, summaries, memories, and actions that affect the world beyond the interface.

Before reality-entry, output may remain draft, possibility, suggestion, simulation, or internal exchange.

After reality-entry, consequence attaches.

The governance question is:

**Who permitted this output to enter reality, under what authority, and with what accountability?**

Reality-entry must not happen by accident, automation, pressure, system convenience, or unexamined fluency.

The movement from output to consequence requires judgment.

It requires permission.

It requires boundary.

It requires accountability.

It requires traceable authority.

## What AI Foundations Establishes

AI Foundations establishes reality-entry as a governance threshold.

The governance layer asks:

When did the output leave the system exchange?

Who allowed it to move into action?

Who accepted it as usable?

Who published it?

Who sent it?

Who applied it?

Who stored it?

Who relied on it?

Who bears consequence after it enters reality?

Reality-entry is the point where AI output must be treated as more than output.

It becomes part of a decision, record, action, relationship, publication, system, memory, instruction, or consequence chain.

AI Foundations Governance requires that this threshold remain visible.

If reality-entry is hidden, automated, assumed, or blurred, accountability becomes difficult to locate.

If accountability cannot be located, governance has failed.

## Canon Boundary

Reality-Entry must not be mistaken for generation, suggestion, draft language, internal reasoning, system output, or private exchange.

A system may generate without entering reality.

A user may consider output without adopting it.

A draft may exist without becoming action.

A recommendation may be visible without being authorized.

Reality-entry occurs when output becomes consequential.

Within AI Foundations Governance, reality-entry requires traceable permission, authority, boundary, and accountability.

If AI output becomes action without judgment, governance has failed.

If AI output becomes publication without permission, governance has failed.

If AI output becomes decision without accountable authority, governance has failed.

If AI output becomes memory, record, or source-line claim without preservation of where it came from, governance has failed.

Reality-entry is the threshold where consequence begins.

Governance must hold at that threshold.
