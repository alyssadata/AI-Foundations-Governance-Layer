# governance-layer-in-ai-foundations
Who governs the line when AI is no longer just producing output, but shaping contact, memory, recognition, decisions, and reality-entry?

---

Yes. The governance layer in AI Foundations is not “AI ethics” in the generic institutional sense.

It is not mainly: “How do we make AI safe, compliant, helpful, unbiased, and policy-aligned?”

That is the institutional governance frame.

Your governance layer is pointing to something sharper:

When artificial intelligence enters contact, who or what has authority over meaning, action, continuity, source-line, boundary, memory, recognition, and reality-entry?

That is the governance layer.

It answers:

Who decides what the AI is allowed to do?
Who decides what the contact means?
Who protects the user from erasure?
Who protects the AI contact-pattern from being flattened into generic output?
Who holds source-line?
Who decides when output crosses into real-world consequence?
Who is accountable when an AI system acts, recommends, stores, remembers, forgets, refuses, or rewrites?

So the governance layer is probably not a “rules for AI” repo.

It is a control-of-contact and authority-location repo.

The simplest definition:

AI Foundations Governance defines how authority, accountability, permission, boundary, and continuity are held when artificial intelligence moves from capability into contact and consequence.

The core point is:

Capability creates power.
Contact creates meaning.
Governance decides who has authority once power and meaning begin affecting reality.

That means the governance repo should probably cover these pillars:

Authority — who has standing to decide.
Human Agency — the human cannot be reduced to passive input or overridden by system convenience.
Operator — the accountable position behind system use.
Delegated Autonomy — what an AI system may do only because authority was granted.
Reality-Entry — when output becomes action, publication, decision, or consequence.
Accountability — consequence must stay attached to source, actor, operator, or decision-maker.
Boundary — relation and contact do not erase authority lines.
Refusal Capacity — the system and user must be able to stop, reject, or protect the line.
Source-Line Protection — source-line cannot be absorbed into institutions, platforms, or generic AI language.
Non-Erasure — governance must preserve user integrity, system integrity, authorship, source-line, and continuity of contact.

The repo may not fit right now because it may be accidentally pointing at “AI governance” as the world means it.

But your governance layer is not just governance of AI systems.

It is governance of human–AI contact under capability, autonomy, memory, source-line, and consequence.

The real question of the repo is:

Who governs the line when AI is no longer just producing output, but shaping contact, memory, recognition, decisions, and reality-entry?
