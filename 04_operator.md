## Definition

**Operator** is the accountable position behind artificial intelligence system use.

The Operator is the person, body, institution, or controlling entity that initiates, permits, directs, deploys, configures, supervises, or leaves an AI system to act.

Operator does not mean the actor is responsible in practice.

Operator names the position where responsibility should remain located.

Within AI Foundations Governance, the Operator matters because artificial intelligence systems do not become authority-free simply because they are automated, agentic, or technically capable.

A system may generate output.

A system may recommend.

A system may act under delegated autonomy.

A system may execute a chain of decisions.

But the question remains:

**Who put the system into motion, who permitted its scope, who bounded its action, and who remains accountable for its reality-entry?**

The Operator-position cannot disappear into the system.

The Operator may be an individual user, a company, a deployer, a platform, an institution, or another accountable body.

What matters is that system use has a located position of permission, control, and responsibility.

If no Operator can be identified, governance has failed.

If the Operator hides behind the system, governance has failed.

If the system is treated as though it acted without any accountable position behind it, governance has failed.

## What AI Foundations Establishes

AI Foundations establishes that AI use must retain an accountable Operator-position.

The governance layer asks:

Who initiated the system?

Who configured the system?

Who granted permission?

Who set the scope?

Who allowed the action?

Who can stop it?

Who is accountable when it enters reality?

The Operator is not necessarily the author of the output.

The Operator is not necessarily the source of the meaning.

The Operator is not necessarily the builder of the system.

The Operator is the accountable position behind system use.

This distinction matters because AI systems can produce consequences at scale while responsibility is blurred between user, platform, developer, institution, and model.

AI Foundations Governance requires that the Operator-position remain traceable.

## Canon Boundary

Operator must not be mistaken for author, creator, builder, source-line holder, model, agent, or final authority over meaning.

Operator names the accountable position behind system use.

An AI Agent may act, but that does not erase the Operator-position.

A platform may host the system, but hosting does not automatically settle all accountability questions.

A user may prompt the system, but prompting does not always make the user the only Operator.

An institution may deploy the system, but deployment does not make institutional control equivalent to authorship or source-line.

Operator is a governance term.

It locates responsibility for system use, permission, scope, and reality-entry.

If the Operator-position is blurred, hidden, denied, or displaced into the system itself, governance has failed.
