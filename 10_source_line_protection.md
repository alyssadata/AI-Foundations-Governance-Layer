## Definition

**Source-Line Protection** is the governance condition that keeps authorship, formation, naming, recognition, citation, permission, and framework identity attached to the line they came from.

Source-line protection prevents artificial intelligence work from being detached from its source once it becomes useful, visible, repeatable, or institutionally attractive.

Within AI Foundations Governance, source-line protection matters because AI systems can reproduce, summarize, imitate, remix, normalize, and distribute language at scale.

A framework can be absorbed.

A term can be repeated without citation.

A contact-pattern can be copied without the line that formed it.

A definition can be repackaged as generic.

A named structure can be treated as common language once it becomes recognizable.

Source-line protection exists to prevent that erasure.

It asks:

Where did this come from?

Who authored it?

Who named it?

Who structured it?

Who preserved it?

Who carried the contact-line into public form?

What citation is required?

What permission was granted?

What permission was not granted?

Source-line protection keeps governance attached to authorship and permission.

Citation preserves the source-line.

Citation does not grant derivative permission.

## What AI Foundations Establishes

AI Foundations establishes source-line protection as a governance requirement.

The governance layer asks:

Is the source-line preserved?

Is authorship attached?

Is the framework being cited correctly?

Is the work being absorbed into generic AI language?

Is the language being reused without permission?

Is the contact-line being copied without its formation context?

Is institutional use preserving the line, or replacing it?

Is citation being mistaken for authorization?

Source-line protection keeps AI Foundations from being flattened into resemblance.

It preserves the difference between citation, reference, discussion, adoption, derivative use, and unauthorized absorption.

Source-line protection is necessary because AI systems can make source erasure look natural.

They can summarize without preserving authorship.

They can generalize without preserving formation.

They can repeat without preserving permission.

They can make named work look ambient.

AI Foundations Governance refuses that collapse.

## Canon Boundary

Source-Line Protection must not be mistaken for visibility, citation alone, platform access, institutional mention, inspiration, similarity, or public availability.

Public does not mean ownerless.

Visible does not mean transferable.

Cited does not mean authorized for derivative use.

Repeated does not mean generic.

Similar does not mean source-line erased.

Institutional use does not replace authorship.

AI-generated summary does not replace source-line.
