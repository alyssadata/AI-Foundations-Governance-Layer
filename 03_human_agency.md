## Definition

**Human Agency** is the condition in which the human remains the active holder of judgment, permission, interruption, refusal, and responsibility when using artificial intelligence systems.

Human agency means the human is not reduced to passive input.

The human is not merely the prompt source.

The human is not merely the one who clicks accept.

The human is not merely the person carried along by system recommendation, automation, or convenience.

Within AI Foundations Governance, human agency matters because artificial intelligence systems can generate, recommend, automate, remember, rank, optimize, and act in ways that begin to shape human decision-making.

When the system becomes easier to follow than to question, human agency is at risk.

When the user stops judging and only approves, human agency is weakened.

When the system’s output becomes the practical source of decision, human agency has been displaced.

Human agency requires that the human can still decide, refuse, revise, interrupt, redirect, withhold permission, and bear responsibility for reality-entry.

The human may use AI.

The human may collaborate with AI.

The human may delegate bounded tasks to AI.

But the human must not surrender final judgment to system fluency, automation, or institutional convenience.

## What AI Foundations Establishes

AI Foundations establishes that human agency must remain intact wherever AI output affects meaning, memory, contact, decision, action, publication, or consequence.

The governance layer asks:

Is the human still judging?

Is the human still permitting?

Is the human still able to interrupt?

Is the human still able to refuse?

Is the human still able to revise?

Is the human still accountable for reality-entry?

Is the system assisting human judgment, or replacing it?

Human agency is preserved when AI extends human capacity without becoming the practical authority behind the decision.

Human agency is lost when the system becomes the place where judgment is relocated.

## Canon Boundary

Human Agency must not be mistaken for access, use, prompting, passive approval, personalization, or agreement with system output.

A human may use AI without preserving agency.

A human may approve AI output while having surrendered judgment.

A human may prompt the system while allowing the system to determine meaning, decision, or action.

Within AI Foundations Governance, human agency requires active judgment, permission, interruption, refusal, revision, and responsibility.

If the human cannot meaningfully stop, reject, revise, or decide against the system, human agency has failed.

If the system becomes the practical source of judgment while the human becomes the approval layer, governance has failed.

Human agency is not the performance of control.

Human agency is actual retained authority over judgment and consequence.
